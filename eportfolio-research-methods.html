Literature Review

Unit 7

Topic

Implementing deep learning techniques in media and entertainment recommendation systems.

Outcomes

What (2)

While general guidance provided useful search strategies, it felt more philosophical for writing (UoEO, N.D.; Ermel et al., 2021). Therefore, I used Dawson's (2015) outline: literature overview, critical evaluation, wider context and gap identification. Although media is my industry, deep learning was new to me. Academic sources like Zhang et al. (2021) provided better technical overviews, whereas industry like Netflix's Steck et al. (2021) delivered superior practical applications of real-life datasets.

So What (3)

Dawson (2015) separated out critical evaluation, so feedback suggesting criticality should be throughout was confusing. Restricting Google Scholar (N.D.) search terms focused my analysis, revealing a recurring research theme of contrasting industry and academic perspectives. Discovering Hammock's (2018) table use simplified analysis and presentation, and graphical representation clarified relationships.

Now What

However, from the feedback I learned to avoid bullet points. Despite the confusion, I'm adding more criticality throughout. Additionally, I find contrasting academia and industry valuable and will continue this.

Feedback

80% (Distinction). 'Outstanding structure and presentation', including visuals. 'The level of knowledge provided is outstanding'. However, add more context to beginning, include 'critical pros and cons' throughout, and avoid bullet points.

Statistical Worksheets

Units 6, 7, 9

Outcomes

What (3)

I found this section frustrating, not because it was difficult, as I had just received a distinction in Numerical Analysis, but because of errors in the material: missing instructions, pages, mislabelled files, and misplaced documents. Another classmate and I reported issues and shared fixes via the WhatsApp group I created for this module. For example, I shared instructions for adding the Analysis group on the Data tab. The documentation also used deprecated Excel formulas, so I used modern equivalents.

Descriptive analytics (unit 6) were straightforward functions. Interpretive statistics (unit 7), like t-tests, required the Analysis group. I extended my analysis with descriptive statistics, a normality plot, and a Jarque-Bera normality test. Despite missing assignment details, I inferred and completed the task. Charts (unit 9) were interesting; I learned new features and created a frequency histogram, again exceeding requirements by creating a box plot to confirm outliers.

So What

The errors and omissions negatively impacted my learning experience. However, solving these issues and exceeding the assignment requirements enhanced my problem-solving skills and strenthened peer relationships. The experience highlighted the importance of accurate and up-to-date instructional materials to facilitate a positive learning process.

Now What

In the future, I can revisit the instructions for Excel statistical analysis or charts. I will continue identifying errors to improve the learning experiences of those who follow and collaborating with peers to improve theirs.

Research Proposal

Unit 10

Topic

The impact of Large Language Models (LLMs) in media and entertainment.

Outcomes

What (3)

My initial task was understanding LLMs in academic literature and demonstrating their application to media and entertainment. Industry created the foundations for LLM with Google's Vaswani et al.'s (2017) paper on Transformers, while academia provided in-depth explanations, like Raiaan et al.'s (2024) LLM literature review. Industry sources like PWC (2023) detailed media and entertainment but not LLMs, while academic papers articulated LLMs with limited research across media and entertainment.

So What (4)

I was surprised that neither Dawson (2015) nor Saunders et al. (2019) plainly articulated the full research proposal methodology. Dawson described project types, while Saunders excelled at strategy. By contrast, my Table 4 diagram provided a clear overview of options and choices. Omission of Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analysis (PRISMA) was surprising, given its common use in literature reviews (Page et al., 2021). The volume of LLM papers, many preprints, was challenging. Using a spreadsheet to track Google Scholar (N.D.) searches and Table 3 to visualise the research gap was essential for structuring the proposal.

Now What

Moving forward, I will use Table 4's comprehensive methodology, aggregating strengths from Dawson (2015) and Saunders et al.'s (2019) with Page et al.'s (2021) PRISMA guidelines. Along with ethical considerations and risk analysis, this will enhance the validity and reliability of my research (Saunders et al., 2019). I will continue using tools like spreadsheets, visuals, and tables to analyse data. I value critically evaluating both industry and academic sources to compare and contrast expertise.

SWOT, Professional Skills Matrix and Action Plan

Unit 11

Outcomes

What

My SWOT analysis captured my strengths as a media technology professional with 30 years' experience and my opportunities for public speaking and service as a student representative and module WhatsApp facilitator. It also reflected my weakness for quality affecting time for self-care, and the threats of course errors and less relevant material (MindTools, N.D.). My skills matrix showed growth in ethics, critical writing, research methods, and statistical analysis in Excel. Notably, I enthusiastically engaged in assignments relevant to AI in media

So What

Despite these achievements, much of the coursework focuses on methodology, writing, mathematics and reflecting, requiring me to seek AI expertise through news and external training. Furthermore, module errors hindered efficiency. Balancing coursework with staying relevant meant dedicating time to less relevant topics, impacting self-care and professional growth.

Now What

My action plan aims to mitigate these challenges (Cottrell, 2021). I will continue to combine industry and academic research, build expertise through public speaking, and supplement with external training. My student WhatsApp group collectively supports and addresses confusion from course errors. However, as I prioritise knowledge, academic quality, and timely delivery, my biggest challenge remains allocating time for self-care.
