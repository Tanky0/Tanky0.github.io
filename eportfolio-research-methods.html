Literature Review

Unit 7

Topic

Implementing deep learning techniques in media and entertainment recommendation systems.

Outcomes

  • 2: While general guidance provided useful search strategies, it felt more philosophical for writing (UoEO, N.D.; Ermel et al., 2021). Therefore, I used Dawson's (2015) outline: literature overview, critically evaluate, wider context and identify gap. While media is my industry, deep learning is new to me. Academia like Zhang et al. (2021) provided better technical overviews, whereas industry like Netflix's Steck et al. (2021) delivered superior practical applications of real-life datasets.
  • 3: Dawson (2015) explicitly separated out critical evaluation, so feedback that criticality should be throughout feels confusing. Restricting Google Scholar (N.D.) search terms helped focus my analysis, with contrasting industry and academia becoming a recurring research theme. Discovering Hammock's (2018) table use simplified analysis and presentation. Furthermore, graphical representation clarified relationships. However, I learned to avoid bullet points, despite their clarity in other literature reviews.

Feedback

80% (Distinction). 'Outstanding structure and presentation', including visuals. 'The level of knowledge provided is outstanding'. However, add more context to beginning, include 'critical pros and cons' throughout, not just evaluation, and avoid bullet points.

  • 7: Literature Review

Statistical Worksheets

Units 6, 7, 9

Outcomes

  • 3: I found this section frustrating, not because it was difficult, as I had just received a distinction in Numerical Analysis, but because of errors in the material: missing instructions, pages, mislabelled files, and misplaced documents. Another classmate and I reported issues and shared fixes via the WhatsApp group I created for this module. For example, I shared instructions for adding the Analysis group on the Data tab. The documentation also used deprecated Excel formulas, so I used modern equivalents.

    Descriptive analytics (unit 6) were straightforward functions. Interpretive statistics (unit 7), like t-tests, required the Analysis group. I extended my analysis with descriptive statistics, a normality plot, and a Jarque-Bera normality test. Despite missing assignment details, I inferred and completed the task. Charts (unit 9) were interesting; I learned new features and created a frequency histogram, again exceeding requirements by creating a box plot to confirm outliers.

    The errors and omissions negatively impacted my learning experience. However, solving these issues and exceeding the assignment requirements enhanced my problem-solving skills and strenthened peer relationships. The experience highlighted the importance of accurate and up-to-date instructional materials to facilitate a positive learning process.

    In the future, I can revisit the instructions for Excel statistical analysis or charts. I will continue identifying errors to improve the learning experiences of those who follow and collaborating with peers to improve theirs.

Feedback

Submitted, but no feedback.
